Reading

In my spare time, I enjoy reading a wide range of books. Often, I focus on exploring a particular theme or subject for a certain period. If you find any topic below that interests you, feel free to reach out to me by email—I’d love to have a chat!

Explanation about the format when listing books.

I list books using the following format:

Author; A (B); Publisher, Publication Year.

When the context is clear, I omit the author's name. Here, A is the book's original title, and B is the title in the language I read. If I read the original version of a book written in English, I list it as "A". If I read the original version of a book not written in English, I list it as "A [B]", where A is the original title and B is the English translation of the title.

Novels of Ishiguro Kazuo

Ishiguro Kazuo (石黑一雄) is one of my favorite writers. The Nobel Prize in Literature’s motivation for awarding him is particularly striking: "in novels of great emotional force, [he] has uncovered the abyss beneath our illusory sense of connection with the world." His exploration of memory, trauma, and consoling, often through unreliable narration, profoundly resonates with me. The delicate yet powerful interplay of these themes is hauntingly beautiful, offering readers the courage to face their own emotional depths.

So far, I have read the following novels by Ishiguro Kazuo:

  • A Pale View of Hills (《远山淡影》); Shanghai Translation Publishing House, 2011.
  • An Artist of the Floating World (《浮世画家》); Shanghai Translation Publishing House, 2011.
  • The Remains of the Day (《长日将尽》); Shanghai Translation Publishing House, 2018.
  • The Unconsoled (《无可慰藉》); Shanghai Translation Publishing House, 2013.
  • When We Were Orphans (《我辈孤雏》); Shanghai Translation Publishing House, 2018.
  • Never Let Me Go (《莫失莫忘》); Shanghai Translation Publishing House, 2018.
Moreover, I have also read some other books about Ishiguro Kazuo and his works:
  • Brian W. Shaffer (Editor), Cynthia F. Wong (Editor); Conversations with Kazuo Ishiguro; University Press of Mississippi, 2008.
  • Yugin Teo; Kazuo Ishiguro and Memory; (eBook) Palgrave Macmillan, 2014.

I used to share my thoughts (in Chinese) on these novels at the library of Northeast Normal University. Besides, I hosted a screening of The Remains of the Day (1993) and Never Let Me Go (2010), which are based on the novels of the same names. These events are part of the reading-sharing sessions at NENU, organized by Prof. Qinghua Liu. Our session's Chinese name is “净月书香·阅读分享”, which means "Bookish charm at Jingyue: A Reading Sharing Session". Here, "Jingyue" is the name of our campus and has the close meaning of "clean moon". Many thanks to Prof. Liu for organizing such a meaningful initiative and offering me the opportunity to share my thoughts!
